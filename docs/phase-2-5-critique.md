# Phase 2.5 Visual Critique

*Before polish — 2026-07-12*

## Diagnosis

The Phase 2 page is **functionally correct** but reads as a **clean academic publisher** rather than a **distinctive editorial-technology product**.

### Hero (desktop)
- Two-column split isolates copy from visual — feels like a template, not one designed scene.
- Excessive vertical padding (`py-28`) creates dead space above the composition.
- The hierarchy card is abstract: floating chips that fade out, with no browser, lecture, transcript, or document context.
- The transformation story (lecture → transcript → structure → summary) is not legible within five seconds.
- Frank Ruhl Libre on the headline makes the whole page feel institutional; serif carries the identity alone.

### Hero (mobile)
- Three stacked cards are informative but not memorable above the fold.
- No recognizable product moment before scroll.
- Headline + CTA compete with weak visual hierarchy.

### CTA & trust
- Primary button is flat study-blue — reads as government/education portal, not premium product.
- Trust line is plain `text-sm` — feels like fine print, not reassurance.

### Header
- Text-only wordmark with no product mark.
- Detached sticky bar — not composed with the hero.
- Navigation and CTA lack deliberate size/weight hierarchy.

### Color
- Muted paper + grey-blue is safe but anonymous.
- No accent system for "live" or "key detected" moments.
- Lacks the digital warmth that signals technology without neon AI tropes.

## Phase 2.5 targets

1. One integrated visual stage (lecture → processing → summary).
2. Headlines in sans (Assistant); serif reserved for summary document zone.
3. Refined palette with Study Marker for key points only.
4. Premium CTA with Chrome icon + install microcopy.
5. Trust line as structured reassurance strip.
6. Prototype monogram in header.
7. Hero fits first viewport; peek of next section below.
8. Mobile: connected vertical transformation above fold.
